HONDA CIVIC 8TH GEN

The eighth-generation Honda Civic is a range of compact cars (C-segment) manufactured by Honda between 2005 and 2012, replacing the seventh-generation Civic. Four body styles were introduced throughout its production run, which are sedan, coupe, and both three-door and five-door hatchback. The sedan version was introduced with two distinct styling for different markets, with one of them sold as the Acura CSX in Canada and as the Ciimo 1.8 in China from 2012 until 2016. The hatchback versions formed the European-market Civic range, which received a different architecture, body design and smaller footprint, and solely produced in Swindon, United Kingdom.

The Type R performance model was introduced in 2007 for sedan and three-door hatchback body styles, with the former only sold in Japan and other limited Asian markets.



NORTH AMERICAN VERSION

In North America, the Honda Civic was available as a sedan and a coupe, designed in Japan and Ohio. The sedan was introduced in September 2005 for the 2006 model year, with various trims including DX, VP, LX, EX, EX with Navigation, and Si. The hybrid was only available as a sedan, and the Si sedan was added to Canada in 2008.

By 2008, new features like a 60/40 rear folding seat and remote trunk release were added to the LX model. The 2009 models had a refreshed front grille. The 2006 models had better fuel economy and lower emissions. The Civic Si and Hybrid had sportier steering, while other models used standard steering.

At the 2011 Auto Show, it was noted that over 1.5 million Civics of this generation were sold, with most made in North America. Production in Ohio ended in early 2009.

Trim details:

  • DX: Basic features; U.S. models lack a standard audio system.
  • VP: Adds air conditioning and a stereo system.
  • DX-G: Adds features like alloy wheels and cruise control.
  • LX: Includes alloy wheels and an anti-theft system.
  • EX: Adds a moonroof and upgraded stereo system.
  • EX with Navigation: Adds voice-activated navigation.
  • LX-SR: Adds a moonroof and other features.
  • EX-L: First to offer leather and heated seats.

Coupes have a sportier design with a rear lip-spoiler. The 2006 Civic Hybrid had better power and fuel efficiency, achieving up to 40 mpg city and 45 mpg highway. It also had low emissions. The Civic GX and Hybrid have similar trims to the LX but include some additional features.



ASIA/JDM VERSION

The eighth-generation Honda Civic has different styling outside the Americas and China. Key differences include:

  • Front Design: More sporty headlights and a front bumper with additional vents and sharper angles.
  • Taillights: Resemble those of the Integra with a distinct design.
  • Interior: Features a two-tone color scheme and a standard three-spoke steering wheel from the American Civic Si and European hatchback.

In the Asia-Pacific market, the Civic is rebadged as an Acura CSX, which includes features not available in American models, like a 2.0L engine, chrome door handles, paddle shifters, and high-intensity discharge headlights. Due to its size exceeding Japanese compact car regulations, the Civic is no longer classified as a compact sedan in Japan, with the smaller Fit Aria taking its place.

Engine options for the Civic include:

  • 1.8L SOHC i-VTEC: 140 bhp, with a 5-speed manual or automatic transmission.
  • 2.0L DOHC i-VTEC: 155 bhp, with paddle shifters available on the automatic version.
  • 1.6L SOHC i-VTEC: Offered in Singapore and Turkey, producing 123 bhp.
  • Hybrid Version: Features a 1.3L SOHC i-VTEC engine with Integrated Motor Assist (IMA).



EUROPEAN VERSION

Designed by Toshiyuki Okumoto, the exterior of the European model more aggressively styled; the sweeping front arc is maintained and a perspex cover resembling a lamp cluster replaces the grille. The rear tail lights are also similarly styled. Triangular accents are found front and back, with triangular fog-lamps in front and dual triangle exhaust tips at the back. The rear glass is split into two levels, divided by a spoiler lip and the fuel filler cap is a race-inspired exposed metal effect cap.

For the 5-door models, the rear door is hidden away with the door handles tucked away at the corners of the rear windows, creating the illusion of a two-door coupe.

  • Design: The European Civic has an aggressive look with a perspex cover replacing the grille, triangular accents, and dual triangle exhaust tips. The rear door handle is concealed to create a coupe-like appearance for the five-door model. The rear glass is split into two sections with a spoiler lip, and the fuel filler cap has a race-inspired metal effect.
  • Suspension: It uses a simpler torsion beam rear suspension compared to the double-wishbone system in other variants, which allows for a larger interior space and reduced length.

  • Interior: The European Civic includes a split-level instrument panel, circular A/C vents, a start button, and metallic trim. It features "magic seats" that can be folded or tumbled to create a versatile load area. The fuel tank is positioned under the front seats to maximize rear floor space. There is also a hidden storage compartment in the boot for added convenience.
  • Features: Standard equipment includes single-zone climate control and heated mirrors/rear windscreen. Optional features across trims include electric folding mirrors, a panoramic glass roof, dual climate control, automatic headlights, xenon lights, headlight washers, heated leather seats, alloy wheels, fog lights, and satellite navigation.



ENGINES

All 8th-generation Honda Civic models are powered by the R18A1 1.8-liter inline-four powertrain with a power output of 140 hp and 128 lb-ft of torque. This is an engine that has been applied to a wide variety of products from the auto manufacturer throughout the years. All pre-facelifted models employ a five-speed manual transmission driving the front wheels as standard, but a five-speed automatic transmission is optionally available. On the final year model, the EX and EX-L trims get the automatic transmission if the navigation package is selected.



1.8-liter inline-four R18A1

The Honda R18A1 is a tried and tested unit consisting of an aluminum-alloy block and cylinder head with a single overhead cam 16 valve configuration employing the brand's i-VTEC technology. The unit's reliable reputation is partly thanks to the integration of a robust cam chain and conventional multi-point fuel injection system. Peak power is available at 6,300 RPM but the engine can spin to an impressive 6,800 RPM redline in true Honda fashion.



K20Z3 i-VTEC

The K20Z3 has Honda's traditional performance VTEC on the intake and the exhaust cams, as found on previous generation of engines. Variable cam timing technology is included on the intake but not the exhaust cams. The added timing control corresponds to the "i" in i-VTEC.
